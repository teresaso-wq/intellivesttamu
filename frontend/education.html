Getting Started

💰

Budgeting Basics

  • Follow the 50/30/20 rule
  • Track spending for 30 days
  • Automate your savings
  • 50% -> Needs (rent, food, bills)
  • 30% -> Wants (dining, fun)
  • 20% -> Savings + debt payoff
  • Best free tools: Mint, YNAB, EveryDollar
🏦

Savings Accounts

  • High-Yield Savings = 4-5% APY
  • Emergency fund = 3-6 months expenses
  • Pay yourself first on payday
  • Best HYSAs right now: Ally, Marcus, SoFi
  • Start with a $1,000 emergency fund goal
  • Then grow to your full 3-month cushion
💳

Credit Score 101

  • 35% is payment history - never miss
  • Keep credit utilization under 30%
  • Check your report free at annualcreditreport.com
  • 300-579 Poor: Get a secured card
  • 580-669 Fair: Pay full balance monthly
  • 670-739 Good: Apply for rewards cards
  • 740+ Excellent: Best rates on loans

Investing 101

📈

Stocks

  • Ownership stake in a company
  • Higher risk, higher potential reward
  • Best for long-term (5+ years)
  • Beginner picks: VTI, SPY, QQQ (ETFs)
  • Individual stocks: AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL
  • Never invest money you need soon
🧺

ETFs & Index Funds

  • Basket of many stocks in one trade
  • Lower fees than mutual funds
  • Great for beginners
  • VTI -> Total US market
  • QQQ -> Top 100 tech companies
  • SCHD -> Dividend focused
  • BND -> Bonds (lower risk)
🏛️

Mutual Funds

  • Managed by professionals
  • Minimum investment often $1,000+
  • Good for retirement accounts
  • FXAIX -> Fidelity S&P 500 (0.015% fee)
  • VFIAX -> Vanguard S&P 500 (0.04% fee)
  • SWTSX -> Schwab Total Market (0.03% fee)
🏠

Real Estate

  • Requires 20% down to avoid PMI
  • Credit score 720+ = best rates
  • Budget 2-5% for closing costs
  • 1) Build credit above 720
  • 2) Save 20% down payment
  • 3) Get pre-approved for mortgage
  • 4) Budget for closing + moving costs
  • Best savings vehicle while saving: HYSA

Market Concepts From Watchlist

📈

Stocks and ETFs Deep Dive

  • A stock is a small ownership stake in a company
  • ETFs hold many assets and trade like one stock
  • Diversification helps lower single-company risk
  • IPO: first time shares are sold to the public
  • Prices move with supply and demand
  • Returns can come from capital gains and dividends
  • Bull market: broad rising prices; bear market: broad declines
  • Volatility measures how sharply prices swing
🧮

Reading Stock Metrics

  • P/E ratio compares price to earnings per share
  • Volume shows trading activity and liquidity
  • 52-week range gives annual context
  • Example: Price $100 and EPS $5 means P/E = 20
  • Dividend yield = annual dividend divided by stock price
  • Chart basics: Open, High, Low, Close, and Change %
  • Market cap = price x shares outstanding
  • Cap buckets: Large-cap ($10B+), Mid-cap ($2-$10B), Small-cap (under $2B)
🏠

Buying a House Checklist

  • Build strong credit before applying
  • Plan down payment and closing costs
  • Compare lenders and loan types
  • Budget for moving costs and a repair buffer
  • Lenders review debt-to-income closely
  • Compare fixed-rate vs ARM mortgage structures
  • Use high-yield savings while building your down payment fund

Taxes, Student Loans & Your First Job

🧾

Taxes 101

  • W-2 = employee, 1099 = freelancer/contractor
  • Standard deduction 2024: $14,600 (single)
  • Contribute to 401k/IRA to lower taxable income
  • File by April 15 every year
  • Free filing: IRS Free File if under $79,000/year
  • Capital gains tax: 0% if income under $47,025
  • Withholdings on your W-2 affect your refund vs. amount owed
  • Traditional 401(k)/IRA contributions may reduce taxable income now
  • Roth contributions do not reduce current taxable income but grow tax-free
  • Short-term vs. long-term capital gains are taxed differently
🎓

Student Loans

  • Federal loans = lower rates, more protections
  • Income-Driven Repayment caps at 5-10% of income
  • PSLF forgives loans after 10 years public service
  • Never miss a payment — ruins credit fast
  • Refinancing loses federal protections — be careful
  • Check your loan servicer at studentaid.gov
  • Keep contact info current with your servicer
  • Auto-debit can reduce interest slightly on some federal loans
  • Document employment carefully if pursuing PSLF
  • Compare refinance offers only if you no longer need federal protections
💼

First Job Finance Checklist

  • Enroll in 401k on day 1 — get the employer match
  • Set up direct deposit split: 80% checking, 20% savings
  • Get renters insurance (~$15/month, always worth it)
  • Understand your health insurance deductible
  • Start emergency fund before lifestyle inflation hits
  • Review your first pay stub — understand every deduction
  • Match = free money; contribute at least enough to capture the full match
  • Emergency fund target: often 3-6 months of essential expenses
  • HSA eligible? It offers triple tax advantages if used wisely
  • Track net pay vs. gross to budget realistically

Quick Reference

📊 Key Investment Terms

  • Bull Market -> Prices rising 20%+
  • Bear Market -> Prices falling 20%+
  • Dividend -> Company pays you cash
  • Portfolio -> Your collection of investments
  • Diversification -> Do not put all eggs in one basket
  • Compound Interest -> Returns on your returns
  • P/E Ratio -> Price vs earnings
  • Market Cap -> Total company value

🎯 Account Types Cheat Sheet

  • 401(k) -> Employer retirement, get the match
  • Roth IRA -> Tax-free growth, best for young people
  • Traditional IRA -> Tax deduction now, taxed later
  • HSA -> Triple tax advantage for medical costs
  • Brokerage -> Regular investing account, no limits
  • HYSA -> High-yield savings, 4-5% APY
