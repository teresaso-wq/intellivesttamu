📊 Budgeting
  • List monthly income (job, aid, support) and all expenses
  • Try the 50/30/20 split: needs / wants / savings + debt repayment
  • Track spending for at least 30 days to see where money goes
  • Automate transfers to savings so you pay yourself first
🏦 Saving
  • Start with a small emergency fund (e.g. $500–$1,000), then grow to 3–6 months of expenses
  • Keep emergency cash in a separate savings account
  • Consider high-yield savings for money you need in the next few years
  • Save windfalls (tax refunds, bonuses) instead of spending them all
💳 Credit Score
  • Payment history is a major factor — never miss a payment
  • Keep balances low vs. credit limits (utilization)
  • Length of history matters — avoid closing your oldest card without a reason
  • Review your credit reports annually for mistakes
📈 Stocks & ETFs
  • A stock (share/equity) is a small ownership stake in a company
  • IPO: company sells shares to the public to raise money
  • After the IPO, shares trade on exchanges (e.g. NYSE, Nasdaq)
  • Prices move with supply and demand; more buyers than sellers often pushes price up
  • Investors can earn via capital gains (sell higher than you bought) or dividends (cash payouts)
  • Prices react to earnings, news, sentiment, industry, and global events
  • Bull market: broad period of rising prices; Bear market: broad declines
  • Volatility: how much prices swing; Diversification: spread risk across many investments
  • ETFs often hold many stocks in one fund and trade like a single stock
  • P/E ratio = price ÷ earnings per share (valuation clue)
  • High P/E can mean growth expectations — or overvaluation
  • Low P/E can mean value — or lower growth expectations
  • Example: $100 price and $5 EPS → P/E of 20
  • Dividend yield = annual dividend ÷ stock price
  • Volume: shares traded in a period; higher often means more liquidity
  • 52-week high/low: yearly range for context
  • Chart basics: Open, High, Low, Close, and Change %
🏛️ Mutual Funds
  • Market cap = stock price × shares outstanding
  • Categories: Large-cap ($10B+), Mid-cap ($2–10B), Small-cap (under $2B)
  • Market cap hints at company size and risk/return tradeoffs
  • Mutual funds pool money from many investors; often actively managed or indexed
  • Compare expense ratios (fees) when choosing funds
🌅 Retirement
  • Contribute enough to get any full employer 401(k) match
  • Consider Roth vs Traditional IRA based on your tax situation
  • Increase savings rate over time (especially after raises)
  • General habits: start early, diversify, long-term focus, research investments, manage risk, avoid emotional trading
🏠 Buying a House
  • Plan for down payment, closing costs, moving, and a repair buffer
  • Stronger credit can help you qualify for better mortgage rates
  • Lenders review debt-to-income — reduce high-interest debt when possible
  • Compare multiple lenders and loan types (fixed vs ARM)
